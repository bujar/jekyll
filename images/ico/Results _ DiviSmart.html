Rental Yield

Rental yield is the ratio of gross annual income to property value. This metric determines an investment opportunity's gross annual return versus initial startup cost. Intuitively, a higher rental yield implies either low property value or good positive cash flows.

Capitalization Rate

Capitalization Rate ("Cap Rate") is the ratio of gross annual net operating income to property value. The Cap Rate is an indicator of how much the investment property yields after expenses on an annual basis (annual return).

Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR)

Debt Service Coverage Ratio is the ratio of monthly rental income to monthly debt expense. The higher the DSCR, the better, since a ratio of 1.00x implies monthly rental income equals monthly debt expenses.

Gross Rent Multiplier (GRM)

Gross Rent Multiplier is the ratio of property value to annual rental income. Standalone, the metric is not very useful, but compared with other properties the GRM can distinguish the best yielding investment opportunity. GRM can also be understood as the number of years a property's rental cashflow will recoup the initial cost of investment; thus, a lower GRM is more advantageous.
